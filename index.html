Jakob Wiedekind

Welcome! I am a DAAD Visiting Assistant Professor of Political Science at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill.

I study international relations and American politics, with a focus on US foreign policy, executive–legislative dynamics, transatlantic relations, and climate change policy. My research examines how presidents exert preemptive pressure on foreign policy legislation and how the power of the purse shapes interbranch dynamics in US foreign policy making. Another strand of my research examines EU responses to rising international uncertainty and increasingly fragile alliances.

My recent work received the Founders Award from the 2026 APSA Section on Presidents and Executive Politics for the best paper by a Ph.D.-holding scholar.

I received my doctorate from Leibniz Universität Hannover (summa cum laude) in 2023, and have been awarded the John F. Kennedy Memorial Fellowship at Harvard University and a Feodor Lynen Postdoctoral Fellowship of the German Humboldt Foundation. I am currently working on a book on legislative politics and the making of US foreign policy, as well as a project on climate change communication and governance.
